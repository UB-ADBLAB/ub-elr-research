CSE 302/303/402 - Section B (Fall 2025)

Course/Program Description

If you're interested in ...

  • Innovative and transformative research in one of the focused area in computer science and engineering?
  • Designing novels software and hardware systems and/or theories for applications beyond current state of the art?
  • Exploring a future career in computer science research?

Welcome to the EL/R (Section B - Research). Through a series of up to three courses (CSE 302/303/402), you may select and contribute to a research project from a wide range of topics and areas and work closely with one of our faculty members who is an expert in those areas as your project mentor. You will also receive practical technical and soft skills training and guidance for research, and guidance of how to get into a research career as a computer scientist.

In this series of courses, you are expected to work with the same team throughout the three consecutive semesters. To continue in a project in subsequent courses, you are expected to demonstrate good performance through the project, including participating in regular meetings and lectures, responding to communications, making reasonable research progress and producing research artifacts (e.g., software/hardware prototypes or proofs and analysis, technical reports/publication and/or presentation).

As part of your degree program:

  • For CS major first enrolled in or after Fall 2023, or has switched to the new course curriculum for Fall 2023, EL/R course series can be used in three ways for the purpose of satisfying degree requirement:
    1. CSE 302 (general upper-level CSE elective)
    2. CSE 302 (general upper-level CSE elective) + CSE 402 (capstone)
    3. CSE 302 (specialization area course 1) + CSE 303 (specialization area course 2) + CSE 402 (capstone)
  • For CS major enrolled before Fall 2023, only one of CSE 302/302/402 may be counted as your CSE 300/400 elective.
  • For CE/bioinformatics/other major, please consult your academic advisor for more information about how this course may be used to satisfy your degree requirements.

Enrollment and Project Selection Process

To join one of the available projects, you need to both be officially enrolled in Section B of one of CSE 302/303/402 and fill out this project preference survey. You do not have to be enrolled first to submit a project preference survey. If the course is at capacity, you have not been enrolled and you are already assigned to a project, please submit email the course instructor and submit a force registration request. Please feel free to make a new submission of the survey if you need to update your information in a previous submission.

You should complete the survey as soon as possible because the project mentors of your preferred projects will need the information to make recommendations for assignment. In any case, please make sure to complete the survey no later than the first day of the semester so we can complete your project assignment on time.

Course Requirements

The main requirements of this course include:

  • Substantially contribute to project through literature survey, research hypothesis and prototype design and/or experimentation and validation.
  • Actively interact with project mentor and teammates to identify challenges, set goals and resolve issues;
  • Participate in basic research training through once-per-week lectures, where topics include research methodologies, soft and technical skill training (e.g., academic writing and oral presentation), technical skill training (e.g., basic tools for producing technical documents, programming, system administration, and visualuzation).
  • Demonstrating research outcome through oral presentation and written reports.

You are expected to work with the same team throughout the entire series of three courses in three consecutive semeseters. To continue in a project in subsequent courses, you need to demonstrate good performance through the project, including participating in regular meetings and lectures, responding to communiations (IM tools/email), making reasonable research progress and producing research artifacts (software/technical reports/presentation). Students with low performance (as determined by the final grade), may not be able to obtain approval for registration request in the subsequent semester.
