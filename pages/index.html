Course/Program Description

Are you interested in ...

  • Innovative and transformative research in one of the focused area in computer science and engineering?
  • Developing novels software and hardware systems and/or theories for applications beyond current state of the art?
  • Exploring a future career in computer science research?

If yes, this is the right place! Welcome to the EL/R (Research) program at the Department of Computer Science and Engineering of University at Buffalo. Through a series of up to three courses (CSE 302/303/402), you may select and contribute to a research project from a wide range of topics and areas and work closely with one of our faculty members who is an expert in those areas as your project mentor. You will also receive practical technical and soft skills training and guidance for research, and guidance of how to get into a research career as a computer scientist.

For CS major first enrolled in or after Fall 2023, CSE 302/303 can count towards your two speciailization requirements, and CSE 402 counts towards your capstone requirement. For students enrolled before that, CSE 302/302/402 count towards your CSE 300/400 electives.

Enrollment Process

During the open enrollment period, faculty members will post a number of research projects with vacancies. To get enrolled in this course, you will need to

  1. Identify one to three projects you are interested in. The available projects for Spring 2025 are listed here.
  2. Fill out this questionnaire form to answer a few questions about yourself (motivation, technical strengths, etc.), and the 1 - 3 projects you'd like to apply to.
  3. Expect one or more emails from the project mentor (the faculty member who supervises the selected project) for interview and/or approval into the project.
  4. If you receive approval from the project mentor, you may submit a force registration into one of CSE 302/303/402. Usually, if this is the first time you're in the program, you should enroll in CSE 302. If you have passed CSE 302, you should enroll in CSE 303. If you have passed both, you should enroll in CSE 402.

Course Requirements

The main requirements of this course include:

  • Substantially contribute to project through literature survey, research hypothesis and prototype design and/or experimentation and validation.
  • Actively interact with project mentor and teammates to identify challenges, set goals and resolve issues;
  • Participate in basic research training through once-per-week lectures, where topics include research methodologies, soft and technical skill training (e.g., academic writing and oral presentation), technical skill training (e.g., basic tools for producing technical documents, programming, system administration, and visualuzation).
  • Demonstrating research outcome through oral presentation and written reports.

You are expected to work with the same team throughout the entire series of three courses in three consecutive semeseters. To continue in a project in subsequent courses, you need to demonstrate good performance through the project, including participating in regular meetings and lectures, responding to communiations (IM tools/email), making reasonable research progress and producing research artifacts (software/technical reports/presentation). Students with low performance (as determined by the final grade), may not be able to obtain approval for registration request in the subsequent semester.
